Images

Images are handled differently depending on if they are fig-images (figures) or dec-images (decorative).

Fig-image

  • Should contain a caption
  • Should have a unique fig id (“#fig-u1_1”)
  • You can reference any fig-image by its unique fig id from anywhere in the course
    • (example: ?fig-u1_1)
  • Should contain alt text
  • Should contain source metadata (at least source-text)
  • May use a lightbox.
    • Adding a lightbox makes the image expandable with a click or tap.

Source: Garvin, D. A, & Roberto, M. A. (2001). What you don’t know about making decisions. Harvard Business Review, 79(8)

Source: Garvin, D. A, & Roberto, M. A. (2001). What you don’t know about making decisions. Harvard Business Review, 79(8)

Dec-image

  • Should not contain a caption
  • Should not have a fig id
  • Should contain alt text as ‘Decorative’
  • Should contain source metadata (at least source-text)
  • May use a lightbox.
    • Adding a lightbox makes the image expandable with a click or tap. Generally this is not necessary with a decorative image.

Quiz Example

We create Quarto quizzes in replacement to H5P quizzes or PDF format. It is manual work, so please send in content early. For next iteration, we will add a carousell format to it.

Please note that the quiz response is not saved. Below is a shortened version. For existing course examples, see LDRS 504 5.2.2 and 5.5.1.

Instructions: For each statement below, rate how often you personally engage in or observe these behaviours in your groups or teams. Use this scale:

1 = Rarely or never
2 = Sometimes
3 = Often
4 = Almost always

1. Listen carefully to others, seeking to fully understand their message, including emotions and intent, before responding.

2. I avoid communication that makes others feel defensive, such as criticizing or controlling language.

3. I am aware of my own emotions during group interactions and try to manage them constructively.

4. I encourage open discussion of different viewpoints, even when there is disagreement or conflict.

5. I welcome and respect minority opinions or dissenting views in group decisions.

How to Interpret Your Results

Step 1: Calculate Your Average Score

Add your scores for all 5 statements and divide them by 5 to find your average.

Step 2: Reflect on Your Average

Average Score Interpretation Reflection Questions
3.5 – 4.0 Consistently Ethical
You regularly practice behaviours that promote ethical and effective group interactions. Keep reinforcing these strengths! What specific habits help you maintain this level? How can you support others in your group?
2.5 – 3.4 Moderate Practice
You sometimes engage in these ethical behaviours but there is room for growth. Which areas do you notice the most inconsistency? What small changes can you make to improve?
1.0 – 2.4 Needs Attention
You rarely or only occasionally use these behaviours, which may hinder ethical group dynamics. What barriers prevent you from practicing these behaviours? What support or resources would help you develop them?

Step 3: Look at Individual Items Identify any statements where you scored below 3. These might be key focus areas to develop, as they represent critical aspects of ethical group interaction.

Step 4: Set a Personal Goal Choose one or two behaviours to focus on strengthening in your next group experience. Consider how you might apply supportive communication, emotional intelligence, or encourage minority opinions more actively.

Reflection Questions (Optional)

  • Which of these areas do you feel strongest in?
  • Which areas could you improve to promote more ethical group interactions?
  • How might you practice supportive communication or encourage productive conflict in your next team meeting?
